Helpful Definitions, Calculations, and Background Information

Helpful terminology and concepts to know when using this app.



Term Definition Calculation
Gross Income total annual income generated by the rental property ($ / night to rent) * (# days rented in a year)
Occupency Rate % of days a rental is rented in a year # days rented / 365 *100
Annual Costs annual costs for the property (i.e. utilities, etc., assumed to be $12,000/year if unspecified) n/a
Property Price house sale price n/a
Net Operating Income represents how profitbable the investment is Net Income = Gross Income - Annual Costs
Capitilization Rate (Cap Rate) helps determine if the investment is profitable Capitilzation Rate = (Net Operating Income / Property Price) * 100


Desirable Capitilization Rate?

Typically investors consider a 15% capitilization rate indicitive of a worthwhile investment.



Example Calculation:

Property: 1436 SE Rex St. 97202 in South Portland
Zillow Sale Price: $630,000
Airbnb Nightly Rental Rate: $97
Gross Income Assuming 100% Occupency = $97*365 = $35,405
Net Operating Income = $35,405-$12,000 = $23,405
Cap Rate = $23,405 / $630,000 *100 = 3.7%
